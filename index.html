Representation Matters
Minority groups are underrepresented and misrepresented in the movie industry.

Background Information

Over time the movie industry has slowly been increasing the diversity in their casts but, it simply is not fast enough. Still, there are many cases of white actors playing the parts of a more ethnically diverse story line. One recent example is in the movie Aloha when Emma Stone plays the character Ng a biracial character (Hawaiian-Chinese-Swedish).

Why you should care

First, many children don’t see people who look like them on screen playing character with careers that the children might dream to take part in. This can reduce the chances children believing they’ll have a future in the given industry. It might also reduce the chance of them exploring various industries because they might only see white men in the various industries. Second, misrepresentation in movies and other medias can lead to increasing stigma to a certain group of people and can promote stereotypes of that particular ethnic group
